Biofilm: A Shared Toolkit

1,078 biofilm marker centroids (bcsC, wza/wzc, pgaAB, glgP) show no concentration in any functional component.

AUROC range: 0.49 – 0.55 (vs. N2 fixation 0.52 – 0.62, carbon fixation 0.52 – 0.65)

Biofilm formation is a general capability — every community carries biofilm machinery. Unlike nitrogen fixation or carbon fixation, it cannot be decomposed into a specialized program.

Key Takeaways

  • Contamination as Ecology — 75% of cyanobacterial genomes harbor stable heterotrophic co-cultures, revealing obligately cooperative metabolic networks rather than isolated producers.
  • Community Archetypes — NMF across three functional layers reveals 8 archetypes dominated by a single core (A7, 72.6% of samples), with rare specialist minorities in distinct niches.
  • Fixer Monopoly Requires Leakage — Metabolic models predict >99.99% cyanobacterial biomass, but empirical data shows 69%. A leakage fraction of 0.31 precisely bridges this gap analytically.
  • Enzyme Budget Tradeoff — Optimal fixer breadth ≈ 3 pathways; beyond this, enzyme cost exceeds self-supply benefit, establishing conditions for stable heterotroph coexistence.
